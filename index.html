The Name of the Wind

My name is Kvothe:
I have stolen princesses back from sleeping barrow kings. I burned down the tower of Treborn, I have spent the night with Felurian and left with both my sanity and life. I was expelled from the University at a younger age than most people are allowed in. I tread paths by moonlight that others fear to speak during the day. I have talked to Gods, loved women, and written songs that make the minstrels weep.
You may have heard of me.

So begins a tale unequaled in fantasy literature- the story of a hero told in his own voice. It is a tale of sorrow, a tale of survival, a tale of one man’s search for meaning in his universe, and how that search and the indomitable will that drove it, gave birth to a legend.

About the author

Patrick Ruthfuss was born in Madison, Wisconsin and studied at the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point. Starting out as a chemical engineer, Ruthfuss jumped major to major until graduating with his Bachelors of English after nine years of study. Ruthfuss would eventually go back to teach English part time while speaking to publishers for his first title known only as “The Book” at that time. A chapter from this book did eventually make its way to the submission files of the 2002 Writers of the Future competition where it took first place. This was the driving force that led Ruthfuss to the necessary contacts to publish the book in three parts, the first released in 2007 called “The Name of the Wind.”
